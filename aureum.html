[ Wearable Technology ]
Bachelor Degree Final Project
Role: Jewelry designer and
program developer
2016


Aureum is a jewelry collection inspired by the filigree of the municipality of Tumaco in the Colombian Pacific Coast, known as the "Pearl of the Pacific" for its majestic natural architecture. The project proposes the creation of an interactive experience based on the use of wearable technology to highlight the importance of this technique used by the artisans of Tumaco. Additionally, it will generate recognition of the practice which can contribute to tourism and cultural awareness of the population.




The idea was to create three different pieces that would each represent a cultural element of the region; music, architecture, and gastronomy. The first item is a neck piece inspired by the skirts of the traditional dresses the women use for dancing el currulao, the main part of the it would be a choker made with filigree. The interaction would be on the clothing added to the choker, and its purpose is to measure the heart rate of the wearer and display lights to the rhythm of the beat of the heart. The second piece will represent the architecture and it's a bracelet that displays sounds of nature with the different vibrations the hands make, like clapping or tapping on a table. The last piece is an analogy to fishing, an important practice for the gastronomy of the region. The headpiece is inspired by the environmental atmosphere that must be taken into consideration when fishing. It detects sound, just like fish do. With a lack of noise, its stones illuminate with green and blue hues that represent the sea.


Different types of Arduino boards were used that could be soldered or sown into the jewelry. For the neck piece, we used an Arduino Lilypad, a pulse sensor and Lilypad LED lights that would be sown directly to the fabric with a conductive thread. The heart rate sensor is then connected to the finger or the earlobe of the user to display the lights. While the Arduino detects the heart rate and reads the pulse, we establish a value for what a normal pulse would be and whenever it would start to go up the lights would gradually start to lighten up more. The red lights resembles the excitement the dancers' carry in their bloodstream. For the bracelet, we used a piezo or vibration sensor that would be attached to the bracelet and connect it to an Arduino Mini Pro. The Arduino would recognize vibrations, like the ones from a clap or a tap on the table, meaning it would read a value given by the piezo. With these values we were able to play a random sound from a sound library. The sounds included the crashing of the waves, the chant of the street vendors, the wind and the birds. Lastly, for the headpiece, we used an Arduino Mini Pro, a sound sensor module, and RGB LEDlights. The sound sensor acts like a microphone and when the sensor detects a signal below the threshold value the lights flash.
